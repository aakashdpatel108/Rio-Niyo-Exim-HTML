Guava

Guava is a tropical fruit, known for its sweet, musky, and slightly acidic flavor, and its edible seeds. Native to Mexico, Central America, and northern South America, this fruit from the myrtle family can be eaten fresh, or used in jams, jellies, juices, and desserts. It's a good source of vitamin C and dietary fiber, supporting digestive health.

Papaya

Papaya (\(Carica\ papaya\)) is a tropical fruit known for its sweet, buttery, orange or red-fleshed pulp and its distinct black, edible seeds. Though it appears on a tree-like plant, it is actually a fast-growing, non-woody giant herbaceous plant. It is native to Central America and southern Mexico but is now cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions worldwide.

Banana

A banana is a soft, sweet, elongated, edible fruit that grows in bunches on the Musa genus of treelike plants. Rich in potassium, vitamin B6, and fiber, bananas offer benefits for heart health and digestion. While the most common type is the dessert banana, a starchy variety known as a plantain is used for cooking.
Quality Guaranteed

Mango

A mango is an edible, sweet, tropical stone fruit from the evergreen tree Mangifera indica, belonging to the cashew family. It features smooth, thin skin that can be green, red, yellow, or a combination, with juicy, yellow-orange flesh surrounding a large, hard, inedible pit.

Quality Guaranteed

Dragon Fruit

Dragon fruit, or pitaya, is a colorful, sweet tropical fruit from a cactus. It has a vibrant, scaly exterior and a white, red, or yellow interior speckled with tiny, edible black seeds. The taste is often described as a mild mix of kiwi and pear, and it can be eaten raw or used in smoothies and desserts
